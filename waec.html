WAEC 2025 Complete Guide for Nigerian Students

Your all-in-one resource for WAEC registration, result checking, timetable, and more

What is WAEC?

The West African Examinations Council (WAEC) is responsible for organizing the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE) for students in Nigeria and other West African countries. It is a major exam for secondary school students in Nigeria who wish to further their education at the university, polytechnic, or college of education.

WAEC 2025 Registration Guidelines

  • Registration starts January 2025 and closes around March 2025.
  • You must register through your school (for internal candidates) or the WAEC website (for private candidates).
  • Registration fee is estimated between ₦18,000 – ₦20,000.
  • Use a valid email and phone number during registration.

WAEC 2025 Timetable

The official WAEC timetable for 2025 will be released in March/April. Always download the official version from WAEC Nigeria.

WAEC 2025 Registration Guide

Registration for the 2025 WAEC examination is expected to begin by December 2024 or early January 2025, depending on WAEC’s final calendar. Whether you're registering as a school candidate or a private (GCE) candidate, the process remains similar across Nigeria.

How to Register for WAEC 2025

  1. Visit an approved WAEC registration center or use the online portal for GCE candidates.
  2. Obtain your registration PIN or token (available via banks, Remita, or WAEC's payment partners).
  3. Provide accurate bio-data: name, date of birth, local government, state of origin, etc.
  4. Select your subjects (you must choose a minimum of 8 and a maximum of 9).
  5. Submit passport photograph with white background (JPEG format, not more than 15kb).
  6. Complete biometric fingerprint capture and thumbprint verification.
  7. Review and confirm your entries before submission — errors can cost you your result.

How to Check Your WAEC Result

  1. Visit the WAEC result portal: www.waecdirect.org.
  2. Enter your 10-digit WAEC Examination Number (e.g., 4310967289).
  3. Select the examination year (2025).
  4. Select the exam type: "School Candidate Result" or "Private Candidate Result."
  5. Enter your WAEC Scratch Card PIN (available at bookstores or online).
  6. Click Submit to view and print your result.

Always ensure your result print-out is saved and emailed to yourself for safekeeping.

Frequently Asked Questions (WAEC 2025)

1. When is WAEC 2025 starting?

The WAEC 2025 examination for school candidates is expected to begin in April or May 2025. The exact timetable will be released on the WAEC official website by March.

2. How can I get my WAEC ePIN?

You can purchase the WAEC ePIN from any commercial bank, Remita, Quickteller, or authorised vendors. GCE candidates can also buy ePINs via the WAEC online registration portal.

3. What happens if I miss the biometric verification?

Biometric verification is compulsory. If you fail to complete it, your registration is invalid and you won’t be allowed to sit for the exam.

4. Can I register for WAEC 2025 without a school?

Yes. As a private candidate (WAEC GCE), you can register directly online without a secondary school, but you must choose an exam center nearest to you.

5. How do I check if my WAEC result is original?

Visit waecdirect.org, input your exam number, year, and ePIN to verify your results. The site also shows your certificate number.

6. What subjects are compulsory for WAEC 2025?

English Language, Mathematics, Civic Education, and at least one science or vocational subject are compulsory for all candidates.

7. Can I combine two WAEC results?

Yes. Most Nigerian universities accept combined WAEC or WAEC + NECO results provided both results meet the course entry requirements.

8. Will WAEC 2025 questions be the same nationwide?

Yes, WAEC maintains a unified examination standard across Nigeria and other West African countries. However, question sets may vary slightly between regions for security purposes.

9. What is the penalty for examination malpractice in WAEC?

If caught, candidates may face disqualification, cancellation of entire results, and legal actions. Schools can also be derecognised.

10. How long is the WAEC certificate valid?

WAEC certificates do not expire. They remain valid for life and are accepted globally.
